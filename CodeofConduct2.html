The PVTN Forums: Code of Conduct
As a member of the PVTN family, you are held to certain expectations regarding your posts and forum behavior. Failure to comply with our code of conduct will result in posts being removed, and users will receive disciplinary actions as outlined below. This applies to posts, comments, and private messages.
  • Be Civil. Do not make abrasive, abusive, derogatory, or demeaning comments toward or about other people. Please be respectful of the fact that people have different experience levels and specialties.
  • Stay active and positive! Above all else, thePVTN is a place to share our knowledge and help up-and-coming techs learn about the industry as well as enjoying the camaraderie of peers around the world. Your engagement in dialogue is what makes our forum strong.
  • Engage in respectful dialogue about site-relevant content. The General forum is the place to start off-topic discussions, and the other forums have designated topics. Starting discussions within the proper forum allows us to have conversations without getting "lost in the weeds" of other topics.
  • No spam, solicitation, collusion, or any other unethical or illegal messages.
  • Report any rule breaking conduct to site administrators. As the site continues to grow, it will be increasingly difficult for us to review every post. If you come across a post or private message that violates our code of conduct, please redirect it to an administrator so we can take care of it.
Thank you for your commitment to an ethical forum group, and we look forward to continuing to talk to you and new members yet to come!

 

Disciplinary actions
It is in the spirit of the Code of Conduct - rather than the letter - that the administrators have set out these basic steps. The administrators reserve the right to accelerate this process depending upon the severity and frequency of violations.
  • 1st offense: warning
  • 2nd offense: 72 hour ban,
  • 3rd offense: 1 week ban
  • 4th offense: month long ban
  • 5th offense: permanent ban
The administrators will not discuss any disciplinary actions taken in any open forum or with any member not directly involved in the violation. We expect our members to respect this.
